Landlord Requires Renters Insurance: What To Do (2026)

Updated: April 2026 · Reading time: 7 min

If your landlord requires renters insurance, you usually need to buy a basic policy, meet minimum liability limits, and provide proof before move-in or lease renewal. Most renters can complete this in one day.

Quick Requirement Checklist

RequirementWhat To Check
Liability limitOften $100,000 minimum in lease terms
Additional insured / interested partyWhether landlord must be listed on policy docs
Proof of insuranceCertificate or declarations page upload/email

Step-by-Step Setup

  1. Read the lease and copy exact insurance wording.
  2. Get at least 3 quotes using same deductible and limits.
  3. Set effective date to match move-in date.
  4. Send proof documents to landlord/property manager.

Common Mistakes

  • Buying policy without matching lease requirements.
  • Wrong effective date (starts after move-in).
  • Forgetting to list property manager details if required.

FAQ

Can a landlord force me to buy renters insurance?

They usually cannot force by law, but they can make it a lease condition.

What if I already have a policy?

Update coverage limits and named party details to match lease terms.
